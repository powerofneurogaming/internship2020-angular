Future of Work Project at UC San Diego

Autism spectrum disorders and related conditions affect at least 1 out of 59 individuals, cost $137 billion per year in the U.S., and result in a staggering unemployment rate for adults that have the cognitive skill and valuable creative talents that can contribute substantially to the technology workforce. The goal of this project is to develop training technology as well as a skill-building internship model to expand access to jobs in the technology sector for neurodiverse young adults.

This work is supported by a grant from the National Science Foundation (#1928604).

PoNG Center at the Qualcomm Insitute

At the Power of NeuroGaming (PoNG) Center at the Qualcomm Institute, UC San Diego, we are building a learning ecosystem around video games and sensor technology. By combining cognitive science, neuroscience, engineering, the digital arts, and education we seek to create games that meet a wide range of goals including medical rehabilitation, behavioral intervention, learning across the lifespan, and encouraging social change.
